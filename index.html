Answered 2 years ago · Author has 242 answers and 97.4K answer views Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire. Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely. It is common for cables to stretch and for air to build up in hydraulic brakes. Make sure your drive train shifts gears correctly. Ride around and shift up and down making sure you can access all your gear combinations. Cable stretching can also make this not work correctly. Your chain will probably need re-lubrication (or even replacement). You will know this if it sounds off as you pedal your bike around. If any of the checks above prove negative, then your bike is not safe to ride. You will need to address the problems yourself or take the bike to a shop for maintenance. Hope this helps, stay safe!
